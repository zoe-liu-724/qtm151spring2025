num_simulations = 2000
sample_size     = 100

vec_xbar = [None] * num_simulations

for iteration in range(num_simulations):
    vec_unif  = np.random.uniform(low = -2, high=2, size = sample_size)
    vec_xbar[iteration] = vec_unif.mean()

vec_xbar

Change in sample size can affect the variability of the distribution. For instance, increasing the sample size can reduce the variability in the distribution, resulting in a narrower spread concentrated around the true mean. On the other side, change in number of simulations does only affects the degree of approximation of the true sampling distribution, in which higher number of simulations can provide a smoother picture of the distribution avoiding fluctuations.
